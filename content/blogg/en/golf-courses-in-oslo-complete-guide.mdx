## 1. Oslo Golfklubb -- Norway's Oldest and Most Prestigious

Founded on 13 June 1924 as Kristiania Golfklubb, Oslo Golfklubb is Norway's oldest golf club with a rich history stretching back nearly 100 years. The course is idyllically situated by Bogstadvannet, just 15 minutes from Oslo city centre.

### Course Information

- **Holes:** 18 (Par 72)
- **Architect:** Steve Forrest (renovated 2009)
- **Established:** 1924
- **Terrain:** Parkland course with dramatic elevation changes

### What Makes Oslo Golfklubb Special?

The course underwent an extensive renovation between 2007 and 2009 costing over 100 million NOK, led by the American architect Steve Forrest. The renovation included the import of 4,500 lorry loads of sand from Denmark and the installation of modern SubAir systems on all greens.

Hole 14 was named "Norway's best par-4 hole" in 2016, whilst hole 12 features a spectacular waterfall right by the green. The course is ranked #3 in Norway by Golf Digest (2022) and #155 on the continent by Golf World (2023).

### Facilities

- Driving range with balls
- Putting green and chipping area
- Golfland simulator centre (7 TrackMan simulators)
- Restaurant "Spiseriet" with panoramic views
- Pro shop and club hire
- New clubhouse from 2020

## 2. Gronmo Golfklubb -- Modern Course in a Unique Setting

Gronmo Golfklubb is beautifully situated on the edge of Ostmarka, near Ryenkrysset. What makes this course particularly interesting is that it's built on the former Gronmo waste deposit -- a unique example of environmental transformation.

### Course Information

- **Holes:** 9 (played as 18, Par 72)
- **Architect:** Peter Nordwall
- **Established:** 2003
- **Terrain:** Parkland/woodland with wide fairways

### Why Choose Gronmo?

The course was designed by the acclaimed Swedish architect Peter Nordwall and, according to Norsk Golf, has one of the best fairway and green standards in Norway. With four different tee options, the course suits players of all levels.

The location on the edge of Ostmarka provides a tranquil atmosphere, whilst the proximity to Oslo city centre (near Ryenkrysset) makes it easily accessible.

### Facilities

- Driving range
- Putting green and chipping area
- TrackMan simulators at Skullerud Park (year-round operation)
- Gronmo Cafe
- Conference rooms and events

## 3. Groruddalen Golfklubb -- Perfect for Beginners

Groruddalen Golfklubb is Norway's only hillside course and offers a unique 9-hole par-3 experience in Tokeruddalen in upper Groruddalen. This is the ideal place for beginners and those looking to improve their short game.

### Course Information

- **Holes:** 9 (Par 27)
- **Type:** Par-3 course
- **Established:** 1988
- **Length:** 1,302 metres (holes vary from 100 to 230 metres)

### A Unique Golfing Experience

The course features Northern Europe's longest par-3 hole at a full 230 metres -- an impressive challenge even for experienced players. The hillside concept provides an entirely different playing experience, and the idyllic setting by Tokerudbekken creates a peaceful atmosphere.

For beginners, this is perfect: you can focus on iron play and putting without the pressure of long par-4 and par-5 holes.

### Facilities

- Driving range
- Putting green, chipping area and practice bunker
- Clubhouse with catering
- Pro shop
- Golf lessons with a teaching professional

## Tips for Golf in Oslo

### Best Time to Play

The golf season in Oslo typically runs from April/May to October. June, July and August offer the best conditions, but spring and autumn can provide quieter courses and lower prices.

### Transport

All three courses are easily accessible by car from Oslo city centre:

- **Oslo Golfklubb:** 15 min (Bogstad)
- **Gronmo Golfklubb:** 15 min (Ryenkrysset/Klemetsrud)
- **Groruddalen Golfklubb:** 20 min (Stovner)

### Booking

All three clubs use GolfBox for booking. We recommend booking well in advance, particularly at weekends and during the summer months.
