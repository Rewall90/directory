## Why Play Golf in Northern Norway?

Northern Norway is not the first place people think of when planning a golf trip. It should be. The region has 18 golf courses spread across Troms, Nordland and Finnmark, and two of them are among [Norway's 10 best golf courses](/blog/best-golf-courses-norway) — Bodø Golfpark at number 7 and Lofoten Links at number 10.

What makes golf in Northern Norway truly unique is the midnight sun. From late May to mid-July the sun does not set. One can tee off at eleven in the evening in full daylight and finish the round after midnight without needing a torch. No other golf destinations in the world can offer that.

But it is not just the midnight sun. In autumn, in September and October, one can combine golf with the northern lights. Play a round at dusk and watch the aurora dance across the sky afterwards. It is a combination that quite literally does not exist anywhere else on the planet.

The landscape is dramatic — mountains plunging straight into the sea, glaciers, white sand beaches and fjords. The courses sit within this landscape, not beside it. Here one plays golf with nature close at hand, with the chance of spotting moose, eagles or seals along the way.

At 69°39' north, at the foot of the Lyngen Alps and roughly 45 minutes from Tromsø city centre, sits the world's most northerly 18-hole course. Tromsø Golf Club was founded in 1996 and opened its 18-hole course in 2002, designed by Swedish architect Jan Sederholm. In addition to the main course, the club has a 9-hole par-3 course — 27 holes in total.

The course has been featured in international media including CNN, and it is not hard to understand why. A 5,800-metre par 71 course with views of the Lyngen Alps, where one can play golf under the midnight sun, makes for a compelling story.

The club has over 550 members, which is impressive for a course this far north. The season runs from May to October — roughly 150 days — and during the brightest period one can play well past midnight.

### Facilities and Green Fees

Tromsø Golf Club has a driving range, putting green, chipping area and practice bunker. The clubhouse has a pro shop and café. There is also an indoor golf centre, Tromsø Golf Centre, with TrackMan simulators expanding to five simulators in 2026. For those travelling by motorhome there is parking with electricity right by the course.

Just minutes from Bodø Airport and the city centre sits Bodø Golfpark, an 18-hole course that combines links golf along the coast with woodland sections further inland. The course was established in 1992 under the name Salten Golfklubb and is [ranked number 7 among Norway's best golf courses](/blog/best-golf-courses-norway) — the highest-ranked course in all of Northern Norway.

The front nine traverse old cultural landscape along the sea. Viking burial mounds and an old boat landing are in play here. The views towards the sea, the island of Landegode and the Lofoten Wall in the distance take many visitors' breath away. The back nine take one into the forest, with an entirely different character — tighter, more technical, and with a stillness that contrasts with the coastal section.

It is this dual character — links and woodland in one and the same round — that makes Bodø Golfpark so special. With 4.7 stars from 70 Google reviews it is clear that players appreciate the combination.

### Green Fees and Practicalities

The green fee is NOK 650 for 18 holes, regardless of the day. Juniors pay NOK 325. The course has a driving range, putting green, pro shop, café and changing rooms. Club set hire with trolley costs NOK 220.

<InfoBox type="info">
  **Near the airport.** Bodø Golfpark is just minutes from the airport. Perfect for a round before
  the flight home, or as a first stop upon landing in Bodø. Consider combining with a trip to
  [Lofoten Links](/nordland/lofoten-links) — the ferry from Bodø to Moskenes takes around 3.5 hours.
</InfoBox>

## Lofoten Links — Arctic Links Golf of World-Class Quality

Lofoten Links hardly needs an introduction. The course on Gimsøya in the Lofoten Islands, at 68° north, is ranked among the world's 100 best golf courses and is one of the most talked-about golf facilities in Scandinavia. True links golf with sand dunes, white beaches, Viking burial mounds and the Norwegian Sea on all sides.

With 4.6 stars from 279 Google reviews and a green fee of NOK 2,000–3,000, this is not just any golf course. It is an experience. People travel from all over the world to play here, and most walk off the course feeling it was worth every krone.

We have written a complete guide to Lofoten Links with a hole-by-hole walkthrough, green fees, midnight sun information and travel advice: [Read our complete guide to Lofoten Links](/blog/lofoten-links-norways-most-spectacular-golf-course).

The course was designed by Jan Sederholm, the same architect as Tromsø Golf Club, and offers 5,884 metres with a slope of 141. Virtually every hole is a dogleg, making accuracy more important than length. The views of the deep blue Frostisen glacier are spectacular. The Skjoma river, famous for its salmon, runs alongside the course. The green fee is NOK 500.

## Midnight Sun Golf — When and Where?

The midnight sun is what makes golf in Northern Norway a truly unique experience. The sun dips towards the horizon but never disappears — and the further north one goes, the longer the phenomenon lasts.

| Location          | Midnight sun period      | Days |
| ----------------- | ------------------------ | ---- |
| North Cape (71°N) | approx. 14 May – 29 July | ~76  |
| Hammerfest (70°N) | approx. 16 May – 27 July | ~72  |
| Alta (69°N)       | approx. 18 May – 24 July | ~67  |
| Tromsø (69°N)     | approx. 20 May – 22 July | ~63  |
| Narvik (68°N)     | approx. 27 May – 15 July | ~49  |
| Lofoten (68°N)    | approx. 28 May – 14 July | ~47  |
| Bodø (67°N)       | approx. 2 June – 10 July | ~38  |

Even outside these periods the nights are light. In practice one can play golf until midnight or later from mid-May to late July across all of Northern Norway.

### Tips for Midnight Sun Golf

- **Bring sunglasses.** The sun sits low and dazzles, especially between 22:00 and 02:00.
- **Book a midnight tee time.** Lofoten Links offers dedicated midnight rates from 19:15 to 02:00. Tromsø Golf Club lets you play well past midnight during peak season.
- **Expect dew.** After midnight dew settles on the grass. Balls roll a bit slower, and one should bring an extra towel.
- **Expect to stay up too long.** There is something about the constant light that makes it hard to stop. Many end up playing 36 holes in a row.

<InfoBox type="info">
  **Midnight sun is not the same as "light nights".** North of the Arctic Circle the sun is
  literally above the horizon 24 hours a day. It is full daylight, not twilight. The difference is
  noticeable — at two in the morning it looks like eight in the evening.
</InfoBox>

## Season and Best Time to Visit

The golf season in Northern Norway is shorter than in the south, but longer than many expect.

| Month     | Conditions      | Notes                                                            |
| --------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| May       | Season opening  | Most courses open in May. Some snow possible early in the month. |
| June      | Midnight sun    | Best possible light conditions. Season in full swing.            |
| July      | Peak season     | Warmest. Midnight sun in the first half. Most tourists.          |
| August    | Still good      | Nights getting darker but still light. Good weather.             |
| September | Autumn + aurora | Lower prices, fewer people. Northern lights season begins!       |
| October   | Season end      | Most courses close. Can be cold but still playable.              |

**Best time for midnight sun golf:** June and the first half of July.

**Best time for northern lights + golf:** The second half of September and early October. Courses are still open, and the aurora can appear in the evening.

**Best overall experience:** The last week of June. Midnight sun at its strongest, summer temperatures, and courses in good condition.

All major golf towns in Northern Norway have airports with direct routes from Oslo.

| Narvik/Harstad    | EVE     | approx. 1 h 40 min     | Narvik GC (1 h drive)        |
| Alta              | ALF     | approx. 2 h            | Alta GC (10 min drive)       |
| Hammerfest        | HFT     | approx. 2 h 15 min     | Hammerfest GC                |

### Car and Transport

A rental car is recommended for flexibility. Distances in Northern Norway are large, but the roads are good and traffic is light. Some distances to be aware of:

- **Bodø → Lofoten Links:** approx. 3–4 hours by car + ferry (Bodø–Moskenes), or fly via Leknes
- **Tromsø → Narvik GC:** approx. 3 hours
- **Narvik → Lofoten Links:** approx. 3.5 hours via E10

<InfoBox type="tip">
  **Combine several courses.** A popular golf trip in Northern Norway: Fly to Bodø → play Bodø
  Golfpark → take the ferry to Lofoten → play Lofoten Links → drive to Narvik → play Narvik GC → fly
  home from Harstad/Narvik. Four days, three world-class courses, an unforgettable trip.
</InfoBox>
